Mathematics
Pre-Calculus • Statistics • 7th Grade Mathematics

Mathematics makes sense. It is filled with patterns and logic that follow set rules and regulations. Unfortunately, the work required to build up a skillset to navigate through problems in this manner is ever-expanding. Innovation is built upon the shoulders of predecessors. This makes the math taught to the next generation more and more dense. We have learned to embrace calculators and graphing programs because the time spent memorizing multiplication tables can be better utilized elsewhere. Students are taught lessons and expected to accept these methods as step-by-step instructions rather than techniques. I hope to guide these students who are filled with so much knowledge to use them as techniques to experiment and discover the relationships that ties math together. Connect statistics to journalism, calculus to music, geometry to art. All of these interdisciplinary connections can only occur after these scholars are comfortable connecting math to math. Begin with smaller connections and build up to the abstract.
I develop my own teaching materials to ensure there is a consistent flow throughout the entire semester and the materials suit the current needs of the students. These are informed by my experience in my undergraduate studies, experience in the classroom, textbooks, and current industry advancements.

Personal Style
To me, understanding is essential. Computer science is a directly applicable field of study, so I like to focus my efforts on self-sufficiency. Through a balance of cohesive lectures that introduce the new concept and practice with its application, students mimic how to gather resources and advocate for their own education.

During lectures, I engage students with open ended questions that provoke discussion and prior knowledge. These are often made from what I observed that they found to be difficult. They are also made to mimic how they should grow and what they should ask themselves to make sure they have a full grasp of the concepts.

I see education as a place where I join the students in exploration of the subject to show that I am on their side and a resource they should use to grow.
