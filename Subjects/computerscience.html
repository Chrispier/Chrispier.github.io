Intro to Computer Science
Objective

As an introductory course to the world of computer science, this course places an emphasis on developing a problem solving mindset within students.

Course Outline
  • Unit 1: Variables and Data
    • Storing values is fundamental to programming. By using variables, values can be saved and modified to be referred by different people and programs. Naming guidelines and understanding limitations of each data type allows for efficient programming. In this unit, students will be introduced to pseudocode with the use of simple operations and variables.
  • Unit 2: Algorithmic Solutions
    • All solutions are not equal, and finding the best solution is what makes a developer stand out. Procedural programming is a basic way of apporaching problems as they are solved step-by-step in order. Although it may not be the most efficient, it does begin developing a problem solving mindset that breaks down a complex scenario into step-sized components.
